      Education

      Myth vs Fact

      Common skincare misconceptions — debunked by Dr. Rajat.

      ✕
      Myth
      "Oily food directly causes acne breakouts."
      Acne is primarily hormonal and bacterial. Diet plays a minor role — high-glycemic foods may worsen it slightly, but greasy food alone won't cause a breakout.
      ✕
      Myth
      "Washing face 4–5x a day clears acne faster."
      Over-cleansing strips your skin barrier, triggering excess oil production. Twice daily is optimal — any more worsens breakouts and causes irritation.
      ✕
      Myth
      "Hair loss is always permanent and irreversible."
      Most hair fall — from stress, nutrition, hormones — is fully reversible with early treatment. PRP and targeted therapy show excellent results in 80%+ of cases.
      ✓
      Fact
      "Sunscreen is necessary on cloudy days too."
      UV rays penetrate clouds and cause pigmentation and ageing year-round. SPF 30+ minimum every single day is non-negotiable for healthy skin.
      ✕
      Myth
      "Natural / herbal remedies are always skin-safe."
      Lemon juice, raw turmeric, and honey can cause chemical burns and allergic reactions. "Natural" doesn't mean safe — always consult a dermatologist first.
      ✓
      Fact
      "Dark skin tones still need sun protection daily."
      Melanin offers some protection but doesn't prevent pigmentation or UV damage. All skin types — from fair to deep — benefit equally from sunscreen.

      How long does acne treatment take?+
      Most patients see significant improvement in 6–8 weeks. Complete clearance for cystic or hormonal acne may take 3–4 months with a customized treatment plan.
      Is PRP treatment painful?+
      PRP is minimally invasive. We apply numbing cream before the procedure. Most patients experience mild pressure at most — not pain. The entire session takes about 45 minutes.
      Do I need to stop my current skincare?+
      Not necessarily. Dr. Rajat reviews your current products during consultation and advises on what to continue, pause, or replace based on your specific treatment plan.
      Are laser treatments safe for Indian skin?+
      Absolutely yes — with the correct settings. The clinic uses parameters calibrated specifically for Fitzpatrick III–V skin types most common in Indian patients.
      What happens in the first consultation?+
      A full skin assessment — Dr. Rajat reviews your medical history, examines the concern in detail, and builds a customised treatment plan. No pressure, no rush.

      What Is Acne?

      Acne is a chronic inflammatory skin condition affecting hair follicles and sebaceous glands. It causes pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, and in severe cases — deep cystic nodules that can scar.

      Causes
      • Excess sebum (oil) production
      • Clogged pores from dead skin cells
      • Bacterial growth (C. acnes)
      • Hormonal fluctuations (androgens)
      • Stress and cortisol spikes
      Symptoms
      • Blackheads and whiteheads
      • Papules and pustules
      • Nodules and cysts
      • Post-acne dark spots
      • Scarring in severe cases
      Treatment Options
      • Topical retinoids and antibiotics
      • Oral medication (antibiotics, isotretinoin)
      • Chemical peels (salicylic, glycolic)
      • Laser therapy for scars
      • Hormonal therapy where indicated
      Treatment Timeline

      What to Expect

      Week 1–2
      Initial purging phase. Skin may look slightly worse. This is normal.
      Week 3–4
      New breakouts reduce. Existing spots begin to flatten and fade.
      Week 6–8
      Significant improvement. Skin noticeably clearer. Confidence returns.
      Week 10–12
      Clear skin achieved. Maintenance plan begins to keep results long-term.

      About Hair Fall

      Hair fall (alopecia) affects millions. Losing 50–100 hairs daily is normal, but beyond that warrants clinical evaluation. Early intervention dramatically improves outcomes.

      Common Causes
      • Androgenetic alopecia (genetics)
      • Iron, B12 or Vitamin D deficiency
      • Thyroid dysfunction
      • Stress-induced telogen effluvium
      • PCOS / hormonal imbalance
      Symptoms
      • Receding hairline (temples, crown)
      • Excessive hair on pillow / comb
      • Visible scalp thinning
      • Widening of hair parting
      • Patchy bald spots
      Treatment Options
      • PRP (Platelet Rich Plasma) sessions
      • Minoxidil topical solution
      • Mesotherapy for scalp nutrition
      • Nutritional supplementation
      • Hormonal therapy where indicated
      PRP Timeline

      PRP Treatment Journey

      Session 2–3
      Hair fall noticeably reduced. Baby hair growth visible at hairline.
      Month 3

      Month 6

      🌑
      About Pigmentation

      Pigmentation occurs when excess melanin forms deposits in the skin. It can affect small patches or large areas and is one of the most common dermatology concerns in Indian skin types.

      Types & Causes
      • Melasma (hormonal, common in women)
      • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
      • Sun damage and freckles
      • Drug-induced pigmentation
      • Age spots (solar lentigines)
      Symptoms
      • Brown, grey, or black patches
      • Uneven skin tone
      • Dark spots post-acne
      • Forehead / cheek discoloration
      • Lip darkening
      Treatment Options
      • Q-Switch Nd:YAG Laser
      • Chemical peels (kojic, TCA)
      • Hydroquinone / Tretinoin Rx
      • Vitamin C + Niacinamide serums
      • SPF 50 as mandatory base
      Treatment Timeline

      Skin Brightening Journey

      Week 1–2
      Treatment starts. Sunscreen becomes mandatory daily.
      Week 3–4
      Peeling or flaking possible. Spots begin to lighten visibly.
      Week 6
      Significant brightening. Melasma fading. Skin tone more even.
      Week 8–10
      Target achieved. Maintenance with SPF and serums begins.

      🍄
      About Fungal Infections

      Fungal skin infections are caused by dermatophytes, yeasts, or moulds. They thrive in warm, moist environments and are highly treatable when diagnosed correctly and early.

      Causes
      • Dermatophyte organisms (ringworm)
      • Warm, humid skin conditions
      • Shared towels, clothes, footwear
      • Weakened immune system
      • Tight synthetic clothing
      Types & Symptoms
      • Tinea corporis (ringworm patches)
      • Tinea pedis (athlete's foot)
      • Onychomycosis (nail fungus)
      • Tinea capitis (scalp)
      • Candidiasis (skin folds)
      Treatment Options
      • Topical antifungal creams
      • Oral antifungal medication
      • Nail lacquer therapy
      • Medicated shampoos (scalp)
      • Lifestyle and prevention advice
      Treatment Timeline

      Clearing Fungal Infection

      Day 1–3
      Antifungal started. Itching and discomfort begin to reduce quickly.
      Week 1–2
      Redness fades. Patch size visibly reducing. Skin begins healing.
      Week 3–4
      Complete clearance in most cases. Skin normalises.
      Prevention
      Lifestyle changes to prevent recurrence. Follow-up if needed.
